Grandma's kimchi jjigae

Made with love, spice, and everything nice

Ingredients

  • 0.5 lbs - nappa cabbage kimchi
  • 0.5 lbs - raddish kimchi
  • 0.25 cup - kimchi juice
  • 4 cups - water
  • 0.5 lbs - pork belly
  • 1 pack - firm tofu
  • 3 - green onions, cut into one inch slices
  • 1 - medium red onion,sliced
  • 3-6 - cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp - korean hot pepper flakes
  • 1 Tbsp - hot pepper paste
  • 2 tsp - sesame oil
  • 3 tsp - anchovy stock powder
  • salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Add sesame oil to the bottom of a large pot.
  2. Heat the oil and add the garlic, green onion, and red onion until softened.
  3. Add pork belly and slightly sear in the pot.
  4. Add the kimchi and cook with the pork.
  5. Add about half of the water and add hot pepper paste.
  6. Cover and let simmer for about 10 minutes on med-high heat.
  7. Add the rest of the water and the anchovy stock powder
  8. Add salt, black pepper, and hot pepper to taste.
  9. Cut tofu and add on top (add it at the end to prevent breaking
  10. Cover and simmer for another 10-15 minutes.
  11. Remove from heat and serve immediately with white rice.

As a second generation South Korean-American, kimchi jjigae was one of those common household staples for my family. Every other week, my mom or grandma always made a huge pot of it on the stove along with a pot of fresh rice when I got home from school. This was one of my favorite meals growing up, and when I eventually got older and moved out, I missed not only my family but all of the delicious Korean food that I always took for granted. I have since tried many times to learn how to cook this dish, and when I visit home I always try to write down my grandma's recipies while she's in the kitchen. While my food will never be as good as theirs, my grandma is always so proud of me whenever I show her what I had made.
